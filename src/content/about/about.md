I'm a Computer Science student at UC Irvine, minoring in Economics and Innovation & Entrepreneurship — a combination that reflects how I actually like to work: **I want to understand a problem well enough to build the right thing, not just the clever thing.** That's why, alongside coursework, I've built projects like an HTTP server from scratch just to understand what frameworks usually hide from you.

That same curiosity pulled me toward AI. Between research on AI-driven game design at UCI's Games+Learning+Society Center and a growing stack of coursework in LLMs, prompt engineering, and agentic AI systems, I've spent the last year figuring out how to turn AI from a buzzword into something that actually ships in a product — like [ScoutStat](/projects), a football analytics platform I built with AI-generated scouting reports at its core.

I've also spent time on the business side of things — including an internship in M&A tax technology — because I think the best technical decisions come from people who understand *why* the business needs them, not just *how* to build them.

Right now, I'm looking for opportunities where I can keep doing both: building real, AI-powered products and thinking seriously about the business behind them.
